# Endorphins: The brain's natural pain reliever

Endorphins are peptide hormones released by the hypothalamus and pituitary gland in response to pain or stress. The term originates from "endogenous morphine"—"endogenous" because the body produces them internally, and "morphine" because they mimic the actions of the opioid painkiller.

About 20 different types of endorphins exist. Beta-endorphin, the most studied variety, is "[associated with the runner's high](https://www.health.harvard.edu/mind-and-mood/endorphins-the-brains-natural-pain-reliever)." The body releases these hormones during laughter, romantic attraction, sexual activity, and while eating enjoyable foods.

## How to release endorphins

Several activities can increase endorphin release:

- **[Exercise](https://www.health.harvard.edu/mind-and-mood/the-no-drug-approach-to-mild-depression).** Moderate-intensity aerobic activity proves most effective.
- **Acupuncture.** Fine needles placed at specific pressure points trigger endorphin release.
- **Meditation.** Deep breathing and mental focus alleviate pain and calm the mind.
- **Sex.** These hormones create feelings of bliss and promote release of love-related hormones.
- **Playing music.** Singing, dancing, or drumming releases endorphins that may increase pain tolerance.
- **Laughter.** Belly laughs alter serotonin and [dopamine](https://www.health.harvard.edu/mind-and-mood/dopamine-the-pathway-to-pleasure) levels.
- **Ultraviolet light.** Sunshine stimulates beta-endorphin release in skin tissue.
